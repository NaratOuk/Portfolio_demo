King Norodom Sihanouk

The man who preserved Cambodia's future

Here's a time line of King Norodom Sihanouk's life:

  • 1922 : Born on October 31 in Phnom Penh, Cambodia.
  • 1941 : Crowned King of Cambodia by French authorities.
  • 1953 : Achieved Cambodia's independence from France.
  • 1955 : Abdicated to pursue politics; became prime minister and head of state.
  • 1970 : Overthrown by a coup; lived in exile.
  • 1975 : Returned as figurehead during the Khmer Rouge regime.
  • 1979 : Fled again after Khmer Rouge’s fall.
  • 1991 : Signed Paris Peace Agreements to end the Cambodian Civil War.
  • 1993 : Restored as king in Cambodia's constitutional monarchy.
  • 2004 : Abdicated due to health reasons; succeeded by his son, King Norodom Sihamoni.
  • 2012 : Passed away on October 15 in Beijing, China.

"King Norodom Sihanouk's life and achievements are a testimony to the far-reaching contributions that one man's unwavering dedication, vision, and leadership can make to a nation's peace and progress"

--King Norodom Sihanouk.
